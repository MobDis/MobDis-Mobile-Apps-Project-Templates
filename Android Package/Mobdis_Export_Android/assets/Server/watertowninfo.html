Welcome to Watertown - Coastal Town of the 21st Century
 
• The first luxurious development that seamlessly integrates residential, shopping mall, and the public transport network – Punggol MRT, LRT and bus interchange.
 
• The 992-unit residential development features four different housing types to cater to different lifestyles – Suites, SOHO apartments, Residences and Sky Patios.
 
Waterfront Shopping
• Residents can look forward to an array of urban conveniences at the integrated retail mall, Waterway Point. The first major retail mall in Punggol, Waterway Point has a wide offerings of entertainment, dining, and retail facilities, including a 1,000-seat cinema.
 
Alongside Punggol Waterway
• The recent opening of Punggol Waterway would allow residents in Punggol easy access to a host of recreational activities. This scenic waterway features a 4.2 km long waterway that offers pockets of greenery, promenades, cycling tracks and water sports where residents can engage in a number of outdoor activities.

Coney Island
 
Cross the bridge to Coney Island, and you can experience its rustic charm on its sandy beaches and quaint woods. The island is also part of the URA's Rustic Coast Proposals, which aims to connect the five coastal areas - Pulau Ubin, Changi Point, Pasir Ris, Coney Island and Punggol Point. The Rustic Park at Coney Island with new recreational amenities will be completed in 2014.

Punggol Waterway
 
The 4.2km Punggol Waterway is the longest manmade waterway in Singapore and the first to cut across a town. The opportunity for a waterway arose from plans to dam up Sungei Serangoon & Sungei Punggol to form water reservoirs. The waterway and the new communal spaces along the promenade will provide an attractive and vibrant waterfront living environment.  It provides more opportunities for water-based recreational activities and sports. The coastal promenade will also be linked up with other future recreational facilities such as golf driving range, plant nursery, a horse riding centre and the Rustic Park at Coney Island.

Sengkang Riverside Park
 
Consists of three open-space land parcels that offers a tranquil experience to visitors. The park is home to a unique eco water feature, a constructed wetland that collects and filters rainwater naturally. The wetland also doubles as a wildlife habitat, where many mangrove birds,  such as the purple heron and collared kingfisher, would visit.
